Rendered with KaTeX

This post uses KaTeX to render LaTeX in the browser. Inline math uses single dollar signs, e.g. Einstein's \( E = mc^2 \), or the Pythagorean identity \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \).

Display equations use double dollars. The quadratic formula:

$$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$

The Gaussian integral is a classic:

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \, dx = \sqrt{\pi}$$

You can also use LaTeX-style delimiters: \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \) inline, or \[ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \] for display.
